## 📝 Module Summaries

### Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
Created a one-page visual infographic using **Canva** covering three key topics: what digital literacy is, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices. The infographic is designed to help first-year students quickly understand essential digital concepts in a clear and visual format.

### Task 2 — Student Digital Portfolio
Set up professional profiles on **GitHub**, **LinkedIn**, and **Kaggle**.
- **GitHub** — Created a profile README with name, branch, year, and learning goals.
- **LinkedIn** — Filled in the Education section with current degree, institution, and expected graduation year.
- **Kaggle** — Created an account and explored beginner datasets and notebooks.

### Task 3 — Coding & Collaboration Platforms
- **Part A (HackerRank):** Completed a beginner-level coding challenge on HackerRank to get familiar with competitive programming environments.
- **Part B (Google Forms):** Built a **Digital Literacy Assessment Quiz** with 8 questions covering firewalls, phishing awareness, strong passwords, source credibility, social media privacy settings, digital footprints, copyright, and cloud computing. Responses are automatically recorded in a linked Google Sheet.

### Task 4 — Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
- Drafted **Email 1:** A formal request to a professor for an assignment submission extension.
- Drafted **Email 2:** An expression of interest to an internship coordinator for a summer internship opportunity.
- Created a **Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist** for responsible online behaviour as a college student.

### Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
Researched and documented a case study on **UPI/Online Payment Fraud** — one of the most prevalent cybercrimes targeting students and young adults in India. Covered how the fraud works, who is targeted, and its consequences. Includes a **Stay Safe Online checklist** with 8 actionable prevention tips including UPI-specific safety measures and official reporting channels (National Cyber Crime Portal and Helpline 1930).
