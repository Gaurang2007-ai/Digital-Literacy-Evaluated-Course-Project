UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs or approving fraudulent payment requests. 
Typically, the attacker contacts the victim through phone calls, messages, or fake links, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or seller.
In many cases, the attacker sends a payment request and convinces the victim that approving it will result in receiving money, while in reality it deducts money from the victim’s account. Sometimes, phishing links are used to collect banking details.
Students and individuals who are not aware of digital payment systems are often targeted. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal data.
This type of fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful handling of online transactions.
